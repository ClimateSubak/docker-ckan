Data Catalogue Roadmap

Why are we doing this?

We believe open data is a key driver of innovation However too often in the climate sector, valuable insights remain siloed within organisations. Climate data is opaque, messy and locked away. We’re looking to change that.

What is the Subak Data Catalogue?

A collection of climate and energy data resources, starting with data created and curated by our Member Organisations, the datasets they use in their work, and expanding to a wider range of datasets into the future. We believe curating datasets that are well documented and clearly connected can accelerate our ecosystem - our Data Cooperative community.

Strictly this site is a metadata catalogue - data about data. We at Subak aren't creating datasets of our own, and we don't want to mirror other folks datasets. What we want to do is catalogue what is already out there, signpost and increase visibility, and increase the quality of what is already being shared. That means more emphasis on metadata standards, machine readability and data quality.

What is Subak?

The world's first accelerator for not-for-profits fighting climate change with data. Read more here. We find and fund individuals and organisations, and help them maximise their impact through our accelerator programme and immersing them in a community of practitioners.

Who is this for?

Anyone who makes use of climate data. Our first users are the analysts, researchers and engineers at not-for-profit organisations fighting climate change. We know there are a multitude of different people who use climate data for a huge range of different tasks and we hope to serve a range of different communities.

How can I add my data?

Create an account and let us know at datacooperative@subak.org what data you would like to share. We can then make you an administrator for a publisher organisation you can use to share datasets.

How is the data licensed? Can I use it freely?

We catalogue data that has been released with open licences as well as more restrictive licenses to give the fullest possible picture of available data. Most published datasets display a reference to the licence under which their owner has released them. This is available on each dataset page. If no licence is mentioned, users should consult any licence conditions that are available on the original portal page or publishing page for the dataset, if available.

Whether or not a licence is available, copyright always remains with the data author unless explicitly given away using for example a CC0 license.

The metadata in Subak's Data Catalogue is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License, where it does not fall under the license of the individual dataset concerned. All of the content on Subak's Data Catalogue (that is - not datasets or metadata), is also released under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.

How did you make the Catalogue?

This website runs using CKAN. In their own words:

CKAN is the world’s leading open-source data portal platform. CKAN is a complete out-of-the-box software solution that makes data accessible and usable – by providing tools to streamline publishing, sharing, finding and using data (including storage of data and provision of robust data APIs). CKAN is aimed at data publishers (national and regional governments, companies and organizations) wanting to make their data open and available.

CKAN is used by governments and user groups worldwide and powers a variety of official and community data portals including portals for local, national and international government, such as the UK’s data.gov.uk and the European Union’s publicdata.eu, the Brazilian dados.gov.br, Dutch and Netherland government portals, as well as city and municipal sites in the US, UK, Argentina, Finland and elsewhere.

CKAN: http://ckan.org/

We have extended the core functionality, leaning on the extensive work of the CKAN community and other users. The code that runs this site lives here: https://github.com/ClimateSubak/docker-ckan/
