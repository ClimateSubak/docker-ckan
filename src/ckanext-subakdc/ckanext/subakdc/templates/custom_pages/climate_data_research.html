    Introduction

    Subak regularly conducts Climate Data Research Initiatives (CDRI's) - periodic deep dives on key neglected climate topics. These initiatives highlight high-impact climate datasets and provide key contextual information on the data landscape for a varity of climate-related topics. We undertake desk-based research and conduct interviews with data producers and data consumers on specific climate topics, asking who (produces data?), the what (data is missing/hard to access?), and the why (this might be?).

    Recent reports

    Power plants: State of the data lanscape

    Feb 2023

    One of Subak's core objectives is to identify, aggregate, and index high quality climate data to facilitate cross collaboration on climate issues, but we can't do it in a vacuum. We interviewed 12 data professionals working for leading energy organisations, and institutions across the globe to understand their perspectives on the power plant data landscape - the who (has the data?), the what (are we missing?), and the why. Unsurprisingly, there's a lot to be improved upon, from data gaps in South and South-East Asia, to better and more persistent data assets, and building better relationships between data users and producers. We highlight these three respective findings in this report, the first of many to come, and offer some key conclusions for the how (we can get there).
